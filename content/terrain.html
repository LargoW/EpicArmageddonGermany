In Epic Armageddon, certain types of terrain have become much more common than others, simply because some types typically contribute to balanced games, while others tend to favor certain types of armies over others. For this reason a revised Terrain Effects table can be found below (player-placed terrain such as Bunkers, Trenches etc. are covered by the Fortified Positions special rule.)

Terrain Effects

Line of Sight: All terrain that blocks line of sight counts as being taller than all units unless otherwise specified. Units that are popped up are able to see beyond such terrain, but can only see targets that are further away from the intervening terrain than they are.

God Machine: Units with the God Machine special rule are so tall that they are considered to be popped up for Line of Sight purposes. If targeted while being behind such cover, they are also considered to be Hull Down from any non-popped up shooting.

Hills and Cliffs: Units that are even partially on a Hill or a Cliff counts as being popped up for Line of Sight purposes.

Hull Down: All terrain that blocks Line of Sight can give Hull Down to vehicles or War Engines that are less than 50% visible to all units in the firing formation./p>

Area Terrain:

Roads: Units that spend a whole move on a road may add 5cms to their move (does not apply to non-standard movement such as counter charges or consolidation movement). In addition, if all of the units in a formation are on a road at the start of their action, you may declare they will make a road action. The formation may attempt to do an Engage, Advance, Double or March action, gaining an additional +1 to the activation roll. However, all of the units in the formation must remain on the road during the entire action.

Skimmers, Support Craft and Impassable Terrain: Skimmers and Support Craft can move over Impassable Terrain, but cannot stop there, not even if the terrain type does not block Line of Sight.

Cover from AV, H-AV and WE: INF units counts as being in cover and receive the -1 to hit modifier (but no cover save) if they are touching a friendly Armoured Vehicle or War Engine, to represent their ability to take cover from enemy fire by crouching behind the vehicle. There can be no more than two units for each Armoured Vehicle, four for each H-AV, or two for each DC that a War Engine has.
