Rules

EA-Ragnarok uses the NetEA rules, which can be found over at the NetEa Tournament Pack site. We have done a few general changes across all Epic Armageddon: Ragnarok lists that we hope will improve the game, and overall list design.

Titans

We wanted Titans to be a more common site in Epic. Looking at NetEA and Epic UK we thought there were a few issues with Titans: They are generally too expensive for the damage output they bring and in many cases they require specialized formations to bring them down, which some armies lack. This leads to situations where the main purpose of the Titan is to deny the enemy BTS, TSNP and DTF by rushing onto the opponents table half, contesting one or both of the T&H objectives. While in som cases very effective, we thought this was not great from a game design perspective.

We have done the following changes to most of the large titans in an attempt to make them a more interesting option.

  • We have reduced the price of most titans to make them more viable, given the importance of activations in Epic.
  • We have introduced a general weakness to being swarmed by enemy infantry. To do this we have reduced the close combat value of many titans and also changed the stats on the Titan close combat weapons. Titan close combat weapons now generally give one extra TK attack doing multiple woulds instead of giving multiple extra TK or Macro attacks. This reduces their efficiency against infantry while still being useful against other War Engines.
  • We have removed Thick Rear Armour from many Titans to force you to play them with a bit more care.
  • We have introduces the "God Machine" and "Split Fire" special rules for large (8+ DC) titans. God machine gives blast markers to your opponents army if you kill his Titan, and Split Fire allows you to target more than one formation under some circumstances.

When combining these changes we hope to see more Titans fielded, but also to see that people design lists to support and protect their Titans in a way that was sometimes not needed in NetEA or Epic UK lists. We also hope this reduces the need to bring specialized Titan killer formations to tournaments.

Knights

We felt that the War Engine rules did not work well when the War Engines were taken in large formations. They block line of sight for each other, both for shooting and fire-fights, and assaults were very challenging if played by the book. Each Knight can only allocate close combat hits to enemies in base-to-base and fire-fight hits to enemies not in base-to-base. To do this correct you would have to roll for each Knight individually, which is a tedious process. In an attempt to fix this we have introduced a new type of unit: Heavy Vehicle. This is used for Knights and similar units like e.g. Stompas. Details on the Heavy Vehicle rules can be found in the army lists, but on a high level they behave like an Armoured Vehicle with 2 DC.

Formation upgrades

Many formation upgrades have received a price reduction to compensate for the loss of activations. We felt that many upgrades were too pricy and were rarely taken given the importance of activations in Epic.
